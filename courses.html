Ontario High School Math & Science Tutoring

Master the curriculum from Grade 9 foundations to Grade 12 university prep. Concept-first learning in Brampton, Caledon, and online.

MTH1W

Grade 9 — De-Streamed Math

A comprehensive foundation in number sense, algebra, linear relations, geometry, data literacy, and financial literacy. Designed for all pathways.

UNIT 1Number Sense & Operations

Students compare, represent, and operate with rational and irrational numbers using mental math and estimation strategies.

  • Rational vs. irrational numbers
  • Integers, fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • Order of operations (BEDMAS/PEMDAS)
  • Scientific notation & Square/Cube roots
UNIT 2Algebraic Expressions & Equations

Simplify algebraic expressions, solve linear equations, and apply the distributive property to model real-life scenarios.

  • Simplifying expressions & combining like terms
  • Solving multi-step linear equations & inequalities
  • Rearranging formulas (e.g., area, speed)
UNIT 3Linear Relations

Analyze linear relationships using tables, graphs, and equations. Interpret slope as a rate of change.

  • Graphing linear relations & calculating slope
  • y-intercept and its meaning in context
  • Slope-intercept form (y = mx + b)
UNIT 4Measurement & Geometry

Solve problems involving perimeter, area, surface area, and volume. Explore the Pythagorean theorem.

  • Area of composite shapes & volume of prisms
  • Pythagorean theorem applications
  • Transformations & Angle relationships
UNIT 5Data Literacy

Collect, organize, and analyze data using statistical measures. Assess bias and draw evidence-based conclusions.

  • Measures of central tendency & spread
  • Data visualization (histograms, scatter plots)
  • Identifying misleading graphs and bias
UNIT 6Financial Literacy

Apply math skills to budgeting, interest calculations, and financial decision-making.

  • Creating and analyzing budgets
  • Simple vs. compound interest
  • Understanding credit scores and debt management
MPM2D

Grade 10 — Principles of Mathematics

Academic preparation for university-bound students. Covers linear systems, analytic geometry, quadratic relations, and trigonometry.

UNIT 1Algebraic Skills & Linear Systems

Solve systems of linear equations using graphing, substitution, and elimination.

  • Review of linear equations
  • Applications of linear systems & word problems
UNIT 2Analytic Geometry

Explore relationships between equations and geometric representations of lines.

  • Slope, midpoint, and distance formulas
  • Parallel and perpendicular lines
UNIT 3Quadratic Relations

Investigate quadratic relations and graph parabolas.

  • Vertex form and standard form
  • Axis of symmetry and vertex
UNIT 4Quadratic Equations

Solve quadratic equations using factoring, completing the square, and the quadratic formula.

  • Factoring trinomials & special products
  • Discriminant and nature of roots
UNIT 5Trigonometry

Explore trigonometric ratios and solve problems involving right triangles.

  • Sine, cosine, and tangent ratios
  • Angle of elevation and depression
UNIT 6Measurement & Geometry

Solve problems involving surface area and volume of 3D figures.

  • Prisms, pyramids, cylinders, cones, spheres
  • Composite figures & optimization
MCR3U

Grade 11 — Functions (University)

Essential preparation for Grade 12 Calculus. Covers functions, transformations, quadratics, exponentials, and trigonometry.

UNIT 1Introduction to Functions

Explore the concept of a function, domain, range, and function notation.

UNIT 2Characteristics of Functions

Investigate key features, intercepts, intervals, and transformations.

UNIT 3Quadratic Functions

Deepen understanding of vertex form, factored form, and completing the square.

UNIT 4Exponential Functions

Explore exponential growth/decay and model real-world scenarios.

UNIT 5Discrete Functions

Study arithmetic and geometric sequences and series.

UNIT 6Trigonometric Functions

Extend knowledge to the unit circle, radian measure, and sinusoidal functions.

MBF3C

Grade 11 — Foundations for College Math

Practical mathematics for college and workplace pathways. Focuses on probability, statistics, quadratics, and personal finance.

UNIT 1Probability

Explore theoretical probability, compound events, and tree diagrams.

UNIT 2One-Variable Statistics

Analyze data using measures of central tendency, spread, histograms, and box plots.

UNIT 3Quadratic Relations

Graph and solve quadratic equations using factoring and the quadratic formula.

UNIT 4Trigonometry

Solve right and oblique triangles using trig ratios, Sine Law, and Cosine Law.

UNIT 5Exponents & Exponential Relations

Explore laws of exponents and model population growth/decay.

UNIT 6Compound Interest & Personal Finance

Apply math to budgeting, credit cards, loans, and financial decision-making.

MHF4U

Grade 12 — Advanced Functions

University preparation for calculus and engineering. Deep dive into polynomials, rationals, trigonometry, and logarithms.

UNIT 1Polynomial Functions

Analyze characteristics, graph, and solve polynomial equations using theorems.

UNIT 2Rational Functions

Investigate asymptotes, discontinuities, and graph rational expressions.

UNIT 3Trigonometric Functions

Model periodic phenomena using the unit circle, identities, and sinusoidal graphs.

UNIT 4Exponential & Logarithmic Functions

Explore laws of logs, graph functions, and solve real-world equations.

UNIT 5Combining Functions

Learn function operations, composition, and inverse functions.

MCV4U

Grade 12 — Calculus & Vectors

Essential for engineering, physics, and computer science. Covers rates of change, derivatives, optimization, and 3D geometry.

UNIT 1Rates of Change

Develop understanding of average/instantaneous rates, secants, tangents, and limits.

UNIT 2Derivatives & Their Applications

Master derivative rules (power, product, quotient, chain) and optimization problems.

UNIT 3Curve Sketching & Optimization

Use derivatives to analyze increasing/decreasing intervals, concavity, and inflection points.

UNIT 4Trigonometric & Exponential Functions

Extend derivatives to sine, cosine, and exponential functions for physics/biology apps.

UNIT 5Vectors in Two & Three Dimensions

Explore vector operations, dot products, and applications in navigation/physics.

UNIT 6Lines & Planes in 3D Space

Represent lines/planes using vector, parametric, and symmetric equations. Solve intersections.

MDM4U

Grade 12 — Mathematics of Data Management

Essential for business and social sciences. Covers data organization, statistical analysis, probability, and distributions.

UNIT 1Organization of Data for Analysis

Learn data collection methods, sampling techniques, and how to assess bias.

UNIT 2Statistical Analysis

Analyze one/two-variable data using measures of spread, correlation, and linear regression.

UNIT 3Introduction to Probability

Explore theoretical vs experimental probability, Venn diagrams, and conditional probability.

UNIT 4Probability Distributions & Predictions

Investigate binomial/normal distributions and expected value for risk analysis.

UNIT 5Culminating Data Investigation

Conduct a major investigation integrating data collection, analysis, and presentation.
